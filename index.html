Autonomous Chess Robot

Our ME101 final project group wanted a challenge, so we built an automated chess robot. The mechanical portion was based on a gantry gripper claw system, using wheels, belts, racks and pinions, Lego, Tetrix and custom models which were 3D printed/laser cut.
The computational chess code is written in Python, using the Stockfish library for move calculation and OpenCV for computer vision move detection. The robot itself runs on RobotC, taking a set of instructions from the laptop and parsing them into the correct chess movement.

Vent Flap Replacement

My mother was complaining that she could not find any reasonably priced vent flaps online to replace our broken ones. So I took a flap that was somewhat in tact, took critical dimensions, and replicated it in Solidworks. I proceeded to print out 15 replacement flaps for around the house using PETG filament for it's UV/temperature resistance. My design is fully functional and saved my parents around $80 in replacements.

Magnetic Whirpool Fishing Toy

Lead a group of 4 in the design of a competitive fishing toy. The premise is to catch the 'fish' of your colour from the whirpool with a launcher and reel, before your opponents. The whirpool mechanism was achieved by motorized spinning of a magnet which spins a magnetic stirrer in the water bowl above it. The launchers were made similarly to pinball launchers, with a pull and release fire. The projectile attaches to the 'fish' and can be reeled in with the 3d printed reel.
