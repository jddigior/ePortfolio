Autonomous Chess Robot

Our ME101 final project group wanted to challenge ourselves building something we love. So, we built an automated physical chess robot. The mechanical portion was based on a gantry gripper claw system, using wheels, belts, racks and pinions, custom models which were 3D printed/laser cut, Lego and Tetrix.
The chess computational code is written in Python, using the Stockfish library for move calculation and OpenCV for computer vision move detection. The robot itself runs on RobotC, taking a set of instructions from the laptop and parsing them into the correct chess movement.

Magnetic Whirpool Fishing Toy

Lead a group of 4 in the design of a fishing toy. The premise of the toy is to catch your colour's 'fish' from the whirpool with a launcher and reel, before your opponents. The whirpool mechanism was designed using the properties of magnets, allowing a spinning motorized magnet to spin another magnet in a bowl above it. The launchers were made similarly to pinball launchers, with a pull and release fire. The projectile attaches to the 'fish' and can be reeled in with the 3d printed reel.

Lithophane pictures

Lithophane is a picture which represents darkness with a thicker wall and brightness with a thinner wall. Therefore, when a light is shone through it, a mono-coloured picture is revealed due to the varying thicknesses. I designed this stand and picture combo in SOLIDWORKS, along with a store bought LED, all as a gift. The final 3D printed product allows easy swapping of lithophane pictures for a customizable experience.
